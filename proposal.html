Group7: Topic Proposal of Porject1 - Color Web App

Problem/Data Domain

When creating their own art pieces, many designers and artists would borrow ideas from the masterpieces' palettes. By analyzing the color choices' expressive and conceptual intention, artists gain inspiration and may put them into their own work. This project aims to display and categorize a gallery of famous paintings by their dominating colors. With our web app, artists could choose the paintings to observe and learn more selectively; they may pick based on their interests in the palettes they like. Other than that, showing the dominating colors could help artists to identify the key tone of the paintings more intuitively. At the same time, our web app also encourages ordinary people to appreciate art better.

Nowadays, numerous existing websites collect masterpiece images, while ranking them and displaying them in tile view. However, not many focus on the problem of displaying masters’ color-selections along with art pieces to give the viewer clearer visual perceptions. Under the masterpiece section, Pinterest exhibits a mass of famous paintings along with their name, year, and painter only. Similarly, the website, Artst, is consistent with the same types of information. Compared with Pinterest, it chooses to include more description of the painting's background. Unlike the previous two, the website Color Lisa displays the artists’ names and provides palettes of their most famous paintings. However, it doesn’t include the painting's image; when clicking on the painting’s name, it would direct users to its introductory page, including more detailed information, which also does not fulfill our request.

App Description

Our target users are all those who love art, especially artists and designers. By interacting with our app, people can further explore ideas in color-selection from masterpieces. We will provide information on masterpieces inside our app, including the main color composition and basic background information. To be more specific, we will extract the main color palette (including the percentage) of each masterpiece using an online color extraction tool (TinEye Labs). At the same time, we will collect the background information of those works from Wikipedia.

The Expected Interactions:

  • On the homepage, our users will be provided with various color palettes (coloring scheme). From there, they can explore different palettes. When the users choose one of them, the web app will show the matched work, which uses this palette as its main color-selection. In this case, our web app will provide users with visual information by clicking the palettes.
  • There will also be a search bar on top of our web app, where users can directly search for the masterpiece they are interested in. In this search bar interaction, users can input either the name of works or the author’s name. The web app will then provide the corresponding work with both the main color-selection information and the background information of that work.

Our web app can provide inspiration and color-selection ideas for artists and designers who want to get examples of how masters use different palettes to create works. Furthermore, we provide two main ways to help our users to get this information. Those who do not have ideas to start can browse different color palettes and get great inspiration from those masterpieces. Those who want to learn a specific work can directly use our search bar to search for the work they are interested in and see how the work implements those colors.
