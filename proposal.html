Group7: Topic Proposal of Porject1 - Color Web App

Problem/Data Domain

When creating their own art pieces, many designers and artists would borrow ideas from the masterpieces' palettes. By analyzing the color choices' expressive and conceptual intention, artists gain inspiration and may put them into their own work. This project aims to display and categorize a gallery of famous paintings by their dominating colors. With our web app, artists could choose the paintings to observe and learn more selectively; they may pick based on their interests in the palettes they like. Other than that, showing the dominating colors could help artists to identify the key tone of the paintings more intuitively. At the same time, our web app also encourages ordinary people to appreciate art better.

Nowadays, numerous existing websites collect masterpiece images, while ranking them and displaying them in tile view. However, not many focus on the problem of displaying masters’ color-selections along with art pieces to give the viewer clearer visual perceptions. Under the masterpiece section, Pinterest exhibits a mass of famous paintings along with their name, year, and painter only. Similarly, the website, Artst, is consistent with the same types of information. Compared with Pinterest, it chooses to include more description of the painting's background. Unlike the previous two, the website Color Lisa displays the artists’ names and provides palettes of their most famous paintings. However, it doesn’t include the painting's image; when clicking on the painting’s name, it would direct users to its introductory page, including more detailed information, which also does not fulfill our request.
